The IPave Process – 5 Simple Steps

At iPave, we make concrete simple. With over 30 years of local Christchurch experience, we've refined a process that's clear, efficient, and built around your peace of mind. From first call to finished surface, you'll always know what's happening and what it will cost — no hidden surprises, just quality craftsmanship you can trust.

Our Step-by-Step Process

From enquiry to completion, here's how we deliver quality concrete work

1

Getting Started

You can bring us your plans for a quote, visit our office and display area at 335 Kainga Road (open six days or by appointment), or arrange for us to meet at your property.

We'll discuss your vision, finishes, and requirements. A detailed quote will then be provided.

Once accepted, we'll agree on a start date and confirm all the details.

2

Preparation

Heights are carefully set to council specifications.

Existing spoil or concrete is removed, and the site is excavated to the correct depth.

Base boxing is installed and compacted.

Drainage may be included to ensure water is collected and managed correctly.

3

Pouring the Concrete

Weather plays a big role in achieving the best results, so we closely monitor conditions before and during your pour.

Surrounding fixtures are protected to prevent splatter.

Our highly skilled in-house concrete placers (with over 100 years' combined experience) pour and level your concrete with precision.

4

Finishing

Once in its monolithic state, the surface is trowelled to harden and then finished according to your needs and council requirements — broomed, sponged, or mag-finished for skid resistance.

Expansion cuts are placed as the concrete sets to reduce natural cracking (timing depends on weather conditions).

For coloured concrete, integral colour is mixed at the plant for a consistent finish.

For exposed aggregate, the surface is treated with rugasol and later washed to reveal the natural stone. A neutralising solution is then applied to clean and protect.

5

Sealing & Protection

Concrete can be sealed the following day.

Sealing enhances the colour, improves appearance, protects against UV damage, and makes cleaning easier.

For long-term performance, we recommend resealing every two years.
